I was

I wanted to say sorry,
but I was too afraid.

I worry —
but all I said was sorry.
Sorry I was,
but cruelty I won’t embrace.

You tried to leave,
and I held your sleeve.

You said goodbye,
and I whispered delay.

I wanted you to say my name,
and I would stay,

for staying would have set me flying,
even if you've grown to loathe me.

I say I am sorry for persisting,
I say I am sorry for remaining,
I say I am sorry for feeling,
I say I am sorry for holding.

I don’t want to die,
for leaving would be my death.

I can feel your eyes blinking,
I can feel your breath whispering,
I can feel your fingers holding,

for what they're holding
is why I am waiting.

A fool you might call me,
but frantic is what I'd call me.

You were,
you are,
you will be...

and I —
I was.
